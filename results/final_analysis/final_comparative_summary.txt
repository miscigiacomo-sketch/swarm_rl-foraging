FINAL COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS
==========================

Included scenarios:
- Exp. 21: 2 agents, fixed obstacles, 5x5
- Exp. 23: 2 agents, random obstacles, 5x5
- Exp. 24: 2 agents, random obstacles, 10x10
- Exp. 25: 3 agents, random obstacles, 10x10

Main findings:
1. PPO stochastic deployment achieved the highest success rate in all included scenarios.
2. PPO stochastic is not equivalent to random exploration. Actions are sampled from the learned PPO policy distribution, not uniformly from the action space.
3. Deterministic PPO became less robust in the most complex 3-agent 10x10 scenario, where the joint action space increased from 16 to 64 actions.
4. The greedy obstacle-aware baseline remained strong, especially in terms of episode length, but it is a hand-coded local heuristic rather than a learned controller.
5. The 10x10 experiments increased the spatial search space and supported the analysis of grid-size generalization.
6. The 3-agent experiment strengthened the swarm-inspired framing by testing scalability to a larger multi-agent system.
